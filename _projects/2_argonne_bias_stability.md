During my summer internship at Argonne National Laboratory, I worked on detector bias stability for the Simons Observatory Small Aperture Telescope under the mentorship of Dr. Katie Harrington. The goal of this project was to understand how atmospheric loading affects TES detector behavior and how those effects propagate into observing performance.

I analyzed thousands of SATp1 observations to study correlations between detector calibration parameters, including voltage bias and fractional resistance, and external observing conditions across wafers, bias lines, and frequency bands. This required building analysis workflows that could compare detector behavior across large observational datasets and identify systematic trends.

**Tools:** Python, large-scale data analysis, detector characterization, observational data pipelines
